Carnivorous Plants

History

Around 70 million years ago, back when dinosaurs still ruled the earth, a genetic mutation allowed for some plants to be able to consume meat, morphing their leaves and root structure. This actually allowed these plants to grow in soil with extremely low nutrient content, because they would be able to get all the nitrogen they needed from their prey. Funnily enough, carnivorous plants don't completely rely on the food they consume for all the nutrients they need, as they are still able to utilize the power of photosynthesis. All these years later, these plants have now spread to every continent in the world except for Antartica, and have developed many unique ways of capturing and consuming prey.

Plant Mechanisms

Over the years, these plants have evolved into their own species and have a multitude of options when it comes to caputring their prey. These include:

Pitfall traps
the prey fall into the stomach
Flypaper traps
prey get stuck to an adhesive
Snap traps
prey become entombed
Bladder traps
prey get sucked in
Lobster pot traps
force prey to move towards their digestive organ
